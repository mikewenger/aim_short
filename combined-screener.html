Dilution risk + recent offerings + short interest + fundamental deterioration, in one ranked report

What this is and isn't: every input is public — SEC filings, FINRA short-interest data, reported financials. This combines four independent, well-known bearish-thesis concepts into one ranking; it is not advance knowledge of any company's plans, and it is not analyst consensus data (see the guidance-cut note below). Nothing here is investment advice.

🔴 Dilution Risk (forward-looking)
  • Has a standing ATM sales agreement (8-K)
  • Cash runway — cash ÷ quarterly burn
  • Active shelf registration (S-3/S-3ASR)
  • Offering frequency, trailing 18mo
🟠 Recent Offering (reactive)
  • Already filed a 424B3/4/5 prospectus
  • Scored by recency of the filing
  • Bonus if price has kept falling since filing
🟣 High Short Interest & Float
  • Short interest ÷ shares outstanding ≥ your threshold
  • Higher % scores higher (10 / 20 / 30%+ tiers)
  • Days-to-cover shown alongside for context
🔵🟤⚫🟡 Fundamental Weakness
  • Falling margins — gross margin, this quarter vs. last (needs ≥$250k quarterly revenue to avoid noise)
  • Decreasing revenue — deceleration or YoY decline (same $250k floor)
  • Debt burden — debt/equity, or negative equity
  • Guidance cuts — best-effort text search of 8-Ks for cut-guidance language; not analyst consensus data (that requires a paid feed) — low recall, absence proves nothing
🟢 Overbought Momentum (15-min chart)
  • RSI(14) ≥ threshold — computed from real 15-min closes, not a daily/1-min proxy
  • MACD bullish — MACD line above its signal line on the same 15-min series
  • Note: raw MACD isn't 0–100 bounded like RSI, so "MACD > 70" isn't meaningful as stated — this uses the standard bullish-crossover reading instead. Say if you meant something else.
